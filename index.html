✓ What makes a good dividend stock?
✓ Yield 2-5% (higher can mean the price dropped — investigate why)
✓ Payout ratio under 60% (sustainable, room to grow)
✓ 5Y growth rate positive (company committed to raising dividend)
✓ Stock in Strong or Neutral Momentum (healthy underlying business)
✓ Above 50MA (stock price in uptrend — total return matters too)
✓ No earnings flag (avoid buying just before earnings report)
Important: This dashboard shows momentum signals based on price data only. It does NOT consider company fundamentals, earnings quality, debt, valuation, or macroeconomic factors. Always do your own research before making any investment decision. Not financial advice.
1
Start with the Overview tab — find Strong Momentum stocks
The three watchlist columns (Strong / Neutral / Weak) give you the first filter. Only consider stocks in Strong Momentum for buying. Neutral and Weak stocks are either going sideways or declining — not the right time to enter. Strong Momentum means the stock is outperforming its peers across 1W, 1M, and YTD timeframes.
2
Check RSI — is it a good time to enter?
RSI tells you whether a stock has been bought too aggressively already.
RSI < 40 Pulled back significantly — good entry zone, trend may resume RSI 40-60 Neutral — reasonable entry, not stretched RSI 60-70 Getting stretched — consider waiting for a pullback RSI > 70 Overbought — the move likely already happened, do not chase
Best entry: Strong Momentum + RSI under 60
3
Check 50MA — is the trend structurally intact?
The 50-day Moving Average confirms whether the medium-term trend is healthy.
^ Above 50MA Uptrend intact — dips are buying opportunities v Below 50MA Downtrend — even short-term rallies can be traps
Rule: Only buy stocks above their 50MA.
4
Use the Signals tab for deeper analysis
Sort the Signals table by Score (descending). For your top candidates check:
52W Position 40-85% is ideal. Under 40% = weak trend. Over 85% = extended, higher risk. vs SPY Positive = beating the market, not just riding it. This is genuine outperformance. EARN flag Earnings coming up — price can move sharply in either direction. Wait until after. +5% flag Already moved strongly this week — may be extended. Wait for consolidation. Trend sparkline Green rising line = steady uptrend this week. Prefer smooth rises over sharp spikes.
5
Check Historical tab for context
Sort by YTD. A stock up +80% YTD already has a lot of gain priced in — much higher risk entry. A stock up +15% YTD with Strong momentum and RSI under 50 is a cleaner, lower-risk setup.
Sweet spot to look for:
YTD gain 10-40%  ·  Strong Momentum  ·  Above 50MA  ·  RSI under 60  ·  52W position 40-80%  ·  vs SPY positive  ·  No EARN flag
✓
When to consider buying
All five conditions below should ideally be true before entering a position. The more boxes you can tick, the stronger the setup.
✓
Signal is Strong Momentum
Stock is outperforming peers across 1W, 1M and YTD. This is your first and most important filter.
✓
RSI is below 60
The stock has not been overbought. There is still room for the trend to continue. RSI 30-50 after a pullback is the ideal entry.
✓
Price is above the 50MA (^ 50MA)
The medium-term trend is up. You are buying in the direction of the trend, not against it.
✓
52W Position between 40% and 85%
Not too close to its yearly low (weak trend) and not too close to its yearly high (extended). The middle range offers the best risk/reward.
✓
vs SPY is positive
The stock is beating the market, not just moving with it. This is genuine outperformance worth backing.
✓
No EARN flag or earnings more than 14 days away
Earnings reports can cause sharp unpredictable moves. Enter after the report, not before, unless you are comfortable with that risk.
Best setup example
Strong Momentum  +  RSI 42 (pulled back, not oversold)  +  Above 50MA  +  52W Position 55%  +  vs SPY +2.5%  +  No earnings flag  +  YTD gain 15-35%
= High conviction entry
Always verify fundamentals independently. A strong momentum score does not mean the company is financially healthy or the stock is cheap.

Signal: Weak Stock has dropped to Weak Momentum — trend broken vs peers v Below 50MA Price crossed below 50MA — medium-term trend has turned down RSI > 75 Very overbought — take some profit, the move is likely overextended 52W Pos > 95% At or near yearly high — limited upside, consider trimming EARN flag If you have a large gain and earnings are in <7 days, consider reducing risk
